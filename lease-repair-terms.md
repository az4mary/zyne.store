Thank you for your question. It is incredibly important that the financial and repair structures of this lease are completely clear upfront.

In the past, I have taken out repair loans in my own name, only to be left financially responsible when a tenant broke the lease. To prevent this, **the "repair now, pay later" financing for this property will be established in your name.** Here is a detailed breakdown of how this structure works, your protections, and the step-by-step financial expectations.

### **1. Financing & Contractor Details**
- **Tenant as the Borrower:** You will be the direct borrower with the financing provider (e.g., Wisetack, GreenSky, or ELMERS). This ensures all terms, amounts, and obligations are clearly defined upfront with absolutely no unexpected changes later.
- **Contractor Choice & Accountability:** The contractor sponsors the loan and is paid immediately. Because they are paid upfront, their integrity is on the line. If the repairs are not done correctly, you have the power to report them directly to the financing company.
    - _Note:_ I am open to using your preferred licensed contractor, provided they offer fair pricing and are in good standing with the financing providers.
-  **Lease & Loan Maturity Alignment:** A mandatory 24-month lease commitment is required. All financing must be structured to be **fully paid off by the end of the 24-month term.**
-  **Lease Duration Commitment:** Because the loan is in your name, **you must be absolutely certain you can fulfill the entire 24-month lease duration**, or be prepared to find someone to transfer the loan to if you must leave early.

### **2. Payment Management & Dual Protection**
To ensure payments are consistently made without adding a burden to you, we will use a **Dual Protection Mechanism**:
- **Landlord as Payment Manager:** Once the lease is active, you will simply pay your total agreed monthly rent to me. I will then manage the account by taking the agreed loan installment out of your rent and paying the financing provider on your behalf.
- **Tenant Visibility & Override:** You will have full visibility into the financing account. If you pay your rent in full and I fail to make the monthly payment to the financier, **the lease legally authorizes you to withhold that loan amount from your next month’s rent.** You can pay the financing provider directly to keep the account in good standing. No landlord approval is needed; you simply provide a written notice and proof of payment.

### **3. Defaults & Application of Funds**
- **Rent Defaults:** If you fail to pay your rent, I will not make the monthly payment to the financing provider. Because you are the borrower, the financing company can bypass standard court or eviction limitations and **go after you directly for collections**.
- **Partial Payments:** If you pay partial rent, the funds will be applied to the **rent first**. Only the remaining balance will go toward the monthly loan payment.

### **4. Summary of Tenant Benefits**
This unique structure is designed to benefit both parties. For you, it guarantees:
- All repair costs are clearly defined upfront.
- **No surprise charges** will appear later.
- **80% of repairs** (based on your priority list) will be completed _before_ move-in.
- **Zero deposit is required.**
- A built-in system ensures your loan payments are made consistently.

## **Step-by-Step Payment Scenario**
To illustrate exactly how this works, let’s assume the estimated repairs from your priority list total **$10,000**, which results in a **$450/month loan payment** for 23 months. (Note: the loan must be fully paid off by the end of the 24-month lease).
**Your rent is locked in at $1,400/month.** Even if the actual repair costs exceed the estimate (e.g., rising to $15,000 or $675/month), **your out-of-pocket rent will never increase.** You will still only pay $1,400/month to the landlord.
Here is the exact timeline of payments:
1. **Lease Signing (April 2026):** You pay the **$1,400 May rent** directly to the listing agent upon signing the lease. No security deposit is required.
2. **Repair Approval:** The contractor provides the invoice. You get instant online approval from the financing company (this does not impact your credit). The contractor is paid within 24–72 hours, depending on their profile.
3. **First Loan Installment (May 2026):** You pay the first **$450 repair installment** directly to the financing company and send proof of this payment to me.
4. **Ongoing Rent (June 2026 onward):** For the remaining 23 months, your only obligation is to pay the **$1,400 monthly rent** directly to me.
5. **Ongoing Loan Payments (June 2026 onward):** Upon receiving your full rent each month, I will act as the payment manager and pay the **$450/month** directly to the financing company on your behalf.
