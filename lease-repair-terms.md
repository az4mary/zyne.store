1. Tenant is directly responsible to the financing provider.
2. The financing provider may pursue Tenant directly for nonpayment.
3. Moving out of the Property does not automatically cancel the financing obligation.
4. Lease termination does not release Tenant from the financing obligation unless the financing provider releases Tenant in writing or the loan is paid in full.

1. Tenant becoming the borrower on the repair financing;
2. Tenant agreeing to the loan payment structure;
3. Landlord agreeing to act as loan payment manager when rent is paid in full; and
4. Landlord agreeing to accelerate agreed repairs before move-in.

If the repair estimate increases, the rent remains $1,400.00 unless the parties separately agree otherwise in a signed writing.

The final loan payment schedule will be attached as **Exhibit B - Loan Payment Schedule**.

1. ____ business days after receiving full rent; or
2. before the financing provider’s due date, if the due date is known.

Landlord is not required to make a loan payment from personal funds if Tenant has not paid full rent.

## 11. Partial Rent During Lease

If Tenant pays only partial rent, the payment will be applied first to rent.

Only funds remaining after rent is fully paid may be applied toward the loan payment.

If Tenant does not pay full rent, Landlord is not required to make the loan payment.

Tenant remains responsible to the financing provider because Tenant is the borrower.

## 12. Tenant Rent Default During Lease

If Tenant fails to pay full rent when due, Landlord is not required to make the loan payment for that month.

Tenant understands that in this situation:

1. Tenant remains responsible to the financing provider;
2. The financing provider may pursue Tenant directly;
3. Landlord may pursue lease remedies for unpaid rent; and
4. The loan default and lease default are separate issues.

## 13. Landlord Misses Loan Payment After Receiving Full Rent

If Tenant pays full rent and Landlord fails to make the required loan payment on time, Tenant is authorized to protect the loan account.

Tenant may pay the financing provider directly and deduct the same amount from the next month’s rent.

Tenant must provide Landlord with:

1. Written notice that the loan payment was missed;
2. Proof that Tenant paid the financing provider directly; and
3. The amount deducted from the next rent payment.

Tenant does not need Landlord’s approval to make this protective payment if Landlord failed to make the loan payment after receiving full rent.

## 14. Landlord Liability for Missed Loan Payment

If Landlord receives full rent and fails to make the loan payment on time, Landlord is responsible for any documented late fees, penalties, or reasonable damages directly caused by Landlord’s failure.

If Landlord’s failure causes negative credit reporting or collection activity against Tenant, Landlord is responsible for reasonable and documented costs necessary to address that harm, to the extent the harm was caused by Landlord’s failure to make the payment after receiving full rent.

Landlord is not responsible for credit damage, late fees, collection activity, or other harm caused by Tenant’s failure to pay full rent or Tenant’s failure to keep the loan current when Landlord had no payment obligation.

## 15. Early Termination With 60 Days’ Notice

Tenant may terminate the lease early for any reason by giving Landlord at least sixty (60) days’ written notice.

If Tenant gives proper 60 days’ written notice, Landlord will not charge accelerated rent or a separate early termination penalty, provided Tenant:

1. Pays all rent due through the termination date;
2. Vacates on or before the termination date;
3. Surrenders the Property empty;
4. Returns all keys/access devices; and
5. Leaves the Property in the same condition as received at move-in, plus any future repairs made by Landlord, ordinary wear and tear excepted.

## 16. Early Termination Does Not Cancel Loan

Tenant understands that early lease termination does not cancel the repair loan.

Because Tenant is the borrower, Tenant remains directly responsible to the financing provider after move-out unless the loan is paid in full, transferred, refinanced, or Tenant is released in writing by the financing provider.

## 17. Early Termination - No Replacement Tenant Yet

If Tenant terminates early and no replacement tenant is paying full rent, Landlord is not required to make loan payments during that vacancy or nonpayment period.

During that period, Tenant must keep the loan current directly with the financing provider.

Landlord will not be responsible for late fees, credit impact, collection activity, or other consequences caused by Tenant failing to keep the loan current during a vacancy or nonpayment period after early termination.

## 18. Early Termination - Replacement Tenant Pays Full Rent

If Tenant terminates early and Landlord secures a replacement tenant who pays full rent, Landlord will resume the payment-manager role from the replacement rent received.

After receiving full rent from the replacement tenant, Landlord will use the agreed loan-payment portion to pay the financing provider or reimburse Tenant for documented loan payments Tenant made during the vacancy or nonpayment period.

Landlord’s obligation under this section applies only after Landlord receives full rent from the replacement tenant.

## 19. Early Termination - Reimbursement to Tenant

If Tenant made out-of-pocket loan payments after early termination because no replacement tenant was paying full rent, Landlord will reimburse Tenant from replacement rent once full replacement rent is received.

Tenant must provide proof of each out-of-pocket loan payment.

Landlord’s reimbursement obligation will continue until Tenant has been reimbursed for qualifying payments made during the vacancy or nonpayment period, but Landlord is not required to reimburse Tenant from personal funds before receiving full replacement rent.

## 20. Lease Completion - Loan Still Current but Not Fully Paid

This section applies only if:

1. Tenant completes the lease term through April 30, 2028;
2. The loan is current as of April 30, 2028; and
3. Loan payments remain due after April 30, 2028 because of the financing provider’s delayed start date or final payment schedule.

In that situation, Landlord agrees to make the remaining loan payments beginning May 2028 until the loan is paid in full.

Under this specific lease-completion scenario, Landlord will make the remaining payments even if Tenant no longer occupies the Property.

## 21. Lease Completion - Loan Not Current

If the loan is not current as of April 30, 2028 because Tenant failed to make required payments, failed to pay rent, or otherwise caused the account to become delinquent, Landlord is not responsible for Tenant-caused late fees, penalties, credit impact, collection activity, or delinquency.

Landlord’s post-lease payment obligation begins only if the loan is current as of the lease completion date.

## 22. Abandonment, Eviction, or Unauthorized Move-Out

If Tenant abandons the Property, is evicted, or moves out without proper 60 days’ written notice, Tenant remains responsible for the loan.

Landlord is not required to make loan payments unless and until Landlord receives full rent from a replacement tenant or another written agreement applies.

Landlord may still pursue any lease remedies allowed under the lease and applicable law.

## 23. Loan Transfer

If Tenant must leave early, Tenant may seek to transfer, refinance, or otherwise restructure the loan, but any transfer must be approved by the financing provider.

Landlord agrees to reasonably cooperate with any lender-approved transfer process, but Landlord does not guarantee that the financing provider will approve a transfer.

Until the financing provider releases Tenant in writing, Tenant remains responsible as borrower.

## 24. Repair Loan Overrun Protection

If the approved repair estimate increases after financing discussions, Tenant’s monthly rent remains $1,400.00 unless the parties sign a separate written agreement.

Any increase in repair cost must be approved in writing before the work is performed or financed.

Tenant will not be responsible for any undisclosed or unapproved repair cost.

## 25. Tenant Access to Loan Account

Tenant must maintain access to the financing account throughout the loan term.

Landlord must be given enough payment information to make payments as payment manager, including the financing provider name, payment portal, account number or reference number, due date, and monthly payment amount.

Tenant must notify Landlord promptly of any changes to the account, payment portal, due date, or financing provider.

Thank you for your question. It is incredibly important that the financial and repair structures of this lease are completely clear upfront.

In the past, I have taken out repair loans in my own name, only to be left financially responsible when a tenant broke the lease. To prevent this, **the "repair now, pay later" financing for this property will be established in your name.** Here is a detailed breakdown of how this structure works, your protections, and the step-by-step financial expectations.

### **1. Financing & Contractor Details**
- **Tenant as the Borrower:** You will be the direct borrower with the financing provider (e.g., Wisetack, GreenSky, or ELMERS). This ensures all terms, amounts, and obligations are clearly defined upfront with absolutely no unexpected changes later.
- **Contractor Choice & Accountability:** The contractor sponsors the loan and is paid immediately. Because they are paid upfront, their integrity is on the line. If the repairs are not done correctly, you have the power to report them directly to the financing company.
    - _Note:_ I am open to using your preferred licensed contractor, provided they offer fair pricing and are in good standing with the financing providers.
-  **Lease & Loan Maturity Alignment:** A mandatory 24-month lease commitment is required. All financing must be structured to be **fully paid off by the end of the 24-month term.**
-  **Lease Duration Commitment:** Because the loan is in your name, **you must be absolutely certain you can fulfill the entire 24-month lease duration**, or be prepared to find someone to transfer the loan to if you must leave early.

### **2. Payment Management & Dual Protection**
To ensure payments are consistently made without adding a burden to you, we will use a **Dual Protection Mechanism**:
- **Landlord as Payment Manager:** Once the lease is active, you will simply pay your total agreed monthly rent to me. I will then manage the account by taking the agreed loan installment out of your rent and paying the financing provider on your behalf.
- **Tenant Visibility & Override:** You will have full visibility into the financing account. If you pay your rent in full and I fail to make the monthly payment to the financier, **the lease legally authorizes you to withhold that loan amount from your next month’s rent.** You can pay the financing provider directly to keep the account in good standing. No landlord approval is needed; you simply provide a written notice and proof of payment.

### **3. Defaults & Application of Funds**
- **Rent Defaults:** If you fail to pay your rent, I will not make the monthly payment to the financing provider. Because you are the borrower, the financing company can bypass standard court or eviction limitations and **go after you directly for collections**.
- **Partial Payments:** If you pay partial rent, the funds will be applied to the **rent first**. Only the remaining balance will go toward the monthly loan payment.

### **4. Summary of Tenant Benefits**
This unique structure is designed to benefit both parties. For you, it guarantees:
- All repair costs are clearly defined upfront.
- **No surprise charges** will appear later.
- **80% of repairs** (based on your priority list) will be completed _before_ move-in.
- **Zero deposit is required.**
- A built-in system ensures your loan payments are made consistently.

## **Step-by-Step Payment Scenario**
To illustrate exactly how this works, let’s assume the estimated repairs from your priority list total **$10,000**, which results in a **$450/month loan payment** for 23 months. (Note: the loan must be fully paid off by the end of the 24-month lease).
**Your rent is locked in at $1,400/month.** Even if the actual repair costs exceed the estimate (e.g., rising to $15,000 or $675/month), **your out-of-pocket rent will never increase.** You will still only pay $1,400/month to the landlord.
Here is the exact timeline of payments:
1. **Lease Signing (April 2026):** You pay the **$1,400 May rent** directly to the listing agent upon signing the lease. No security deposit is required.
2. **Repair Approval:** The contractor provides the invoice. You get instant online approval from the financing company (this does not impact your credit). The contractor is paid within 24–72 hours, depending on their profile.
3. **First Loan Installment (May 2026):** You pay the first **$450 repair installment** directly to the financing company and send proof of this payment to me.
4. **Ongoing Rent (June 2026 onward):** For the remaining 23 months, your only obligation is to pay the **$1,400 monthly rent** directly to me.
5. **Ongoing Loan Payments (June 2026 onward):** Upon receiving your full rent each month, I will act as the payment manager and pay the **$450/month** directly to the financing company on your behalf.
